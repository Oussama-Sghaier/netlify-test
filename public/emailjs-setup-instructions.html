EmailJS Setup Instructions for HCELL Engineering Contact Form

This document provides step-by-step instructions for setting up EmailJS to make your contact form functional.

Note: This file is for your reference only and should not be uploaded to your public website.

Step 1: Create an EmailJS Account

  1. Go to https://www.emailjs.com/ and sign up for a free account.
  2. The free plan allows 200 emails per month, which should be sufficient for most small business websites.

Step 2: Create an Email Service

  1. In your EmailJS dashboard, go to "Email Services" and click "Add New Service".
  2. Choose your email provider (Gmail, Outlook, etc.) and follow the instructions to connect your account.
  3. Name your service (e.g., "HCELL Contact Form") and save it.
  4. Note down the Service ID - you'll need this later.

Step 3: Create an Email Template

  1. Go to "Email Templates" and click "Create New Template".
  2. Name your template (e.g., "HCELL Contact Form Template").
  3. For the best results, use our pre-designed HTML template:
    1. Download the email-template.html file from your project.
    2. In EmailJS, select "Create Template" → "From Scratch".
    3. Click on the "Code" tab in the template editor.
    4. Copy and paste the entire content of email-template.html into the code editor.
    5. Click "Save" to save your template.
  4. Note down the Template ID.

Step 4: Get Your Public Key

  1. Go to "Account" → "API Keys".
  2. Copy your Public Key.

Step 5: Update Your Code

  1. Open the app/contact/page.tsx file in your project.
  2. Replace the following placeholders with your actual values:
    • Replace "YOUR_PUBLIC_KEY" with your EmailJS Public Key
    • Replace "YOUR_SERVICE_ID" with your EmailJS Service ID
    • Replace "YOUR_TEMPLATE_ID" with your EmailJS Template ID

Step 6: Test Your Form

  1. After deploying your website, test the contact form by submitting a test message.
  2. Check your email to ensure you receive the form submission correctly.
  3. Verify that the success message appears after submission.

Troubleshooting

  • If emails are not being received, check your spam folder.
  • Verify that all IDs and keys are correctly entered in the code.
  • Check the EmailJS dashboard for any error logs.
  • If you exceed the free tier limit (200 emails/month), you'll need to upgrade your EmailJS plan.
Security Note: Your EmailJS Public Key is designed to be used on the client side and is safe to include in your code. However, never share your Private Key.
